FAQ

What is a hackathon?

Hackathons are where your crazy idea becomes reality. Students work in teams for 24 hours to create something amazing. Build anything—there are no restrictions on what you can create. Dive in and make the most incredible thing you can alongside fellow creators. While some may see hackers as the crazy ones, we see genius.

Who can attend?

All high school students from any high school are eligible. We are also welcoming aspiring middle school students on a limited basis?

What if I don't know how to code?

All students regardless of coding ability are welcome. Hackathons are a great place to learn, so come and build your first app or website! We'll also have workshops for you to learn something new.

What if I don't have a team or an idea?

No problem! Many hackers do not have a fully-formed team or hack concept before the event begins. We will be hosting team-forming activities after hacking begins to help you find the perfect match. What can I build?

Web, mobile, hardware, you name it. Any type of project is welcome. Projects will be judged based on creativity, technical difficulty, polish, and utility by a panel of industry judges.

Who can attend?

All high school students from any high school are eligible. We are also welcoming aspiring middle school students on a limited basis?

How big should teams be? Can I hack alone?

We highly encourage you to build with a team. Teamwork is deeply rooted in the hacking ethos, and you'll have a better time building with your brightest peers. Teams should be at most five people.

How much does it cost?

Cal Hacks is free for all accepted hackers. We will provide your WiFi, meals, swag, and workspace for the entire weekend.

What should I bring?

All attendees are required to bring their valid student ID (or equivalent school ID) for admission. Bring laptops, phones, chargers, and any other electronics you need for the weekend. Sleeping bags, and toiletries are highly recommended.
